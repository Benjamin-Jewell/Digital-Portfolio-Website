Professional Development Journal

The following is a journal of the progression made during the first year of study of the BSc (Hons) Computing and Digital Technologies course, each entry reflects on what I have learnt so far, and what I need to develop further.


Entry 1

During this period, I underwent my introduction to university, and the modules on this course. The modules include: Programming and Software Fundamentals, Web Application Development, Cyber Security Fundamentals, Network Fundamentals and Design, and Principles of Data Science. The focus of these first two weeks was mainly on introductions to the fundamental concepts of each module, with some modules progressing to the main content by the second week. Out of these modules, there are some that I am more confident in than others; I have touched on the basics of each one before, however there are a few I do not have in-depth knowledge about. Because of my Digital Production, Design and Development T-Level before this, I am already confident in my skills of programming and website development, however, cyber security and network fundamentals and design are areas I am the least confident in, since I do not have much experience in them at all.



Entry 2

This period focused on expanding upon the basics of each module. For example, within programming and software fundamentals, we developed skills in variables, data types, and type conversion, as well as control flow (if statements), and in data science we explored further in data structures, and database management systems. So far, I am comfortable in each module since I am mostly familiar with the basics of each one, however I am sure that in the future, as the depth of the modules expands, I will have to challenge myself further to ensure that I fully understand the subjects that I am less experienced in.



Entry 3

Throughout this period, the depth of each module has expanded, and the first assignment has been released. In web development, we have reached the point of starting to create websites using HTML and CSS, with the fundamental concepts of each (of which I am already familiar with) being the main focus of these few weeks. The first assignment was released, this assignment is in cyber security fundamentals, and the main focus is to apply the knowledge we’ve gained so far. This is because the assignment is about creating a seminar about cyber threat actors and attack vectors, as well as mitigation tools and techniques. I am fairly confident how well I can create this seminar, however since I have never done any kind of presentation before, I am quite nervous about presenting it to an audience. However, I am sure this will expand my skillset, and I want to grow more confident in presenting to an audience.



Entry 4

At this time, the modules are now being taught at their full depth, and the next two assignments released. In data science we moved on to look at SQL databases and commands, which I have done a small amount of in the past, however refreshing my knowledge of SQL was very helpful. The network fundamentals assignment requires me to select networking features (architecture, topology, principles) for a fictional scenario, as well perform appraisals of networking protocols. I also had to create a network model for the scenario using a program called cisco packet tracer, for accurate, functional network designs. The data science assignment requires me to create a presentation covering the fundamental concepts of data science, including: the data lifecycle, fundamentals of data storage, challenges in maintaining data quality, and more. I am fairly confident in my skills required for these assignments, however I will research further into the networking one, since that is where my abilities are weakest at this point.



Entry 5

In this period, the final assignments were released. Within the modules, few topics were covered at this time, since the time that would have been spent on said topics, was instead spent completing assignments. The assignment that released was the programming assignment, which required me to produce a portfolio of programming solutions to five given problems, with two different methods of solving each problem, and the web development assignment, which required me to perform a critical analysis of an existing website, and then create a presentation of the findings. I am confident in my ability to complete these assignments, as programming, as well as web development are the areas I have the most knowledge and skills in.



Entry 6

The focus of this period was mainly on completing the assignments, so very little modules had taught sessions, and instead had their time dedicated to completing assignments. I am confident in how each assignment is going, however I am unsure of a few specifics, this includes referencing. This is because I haven’t had to do any kind of academic writing before this, and therefore finding reliable academic references is a new area for me. However, thanks to the professional skills module, there is guidance for this area for me.



Entry 7

With all the assignments of the first wave now finished, the modules have moved back on to taught topics. This includes data visualisation for data science, and object-oriented programming for programming and software fundamentals. I have done a small amount of data visualisation before, however object-oriented programming is new to me, so despite being confident in programming already, it is beginning to pose a challenge the more depth is explored within the subject. Despite this, I am still confident in my abilities in programming and will continue to pursue knowledge and skills in order to improve.



Entry 8

Within this period, the first assignments of the second wave were released. These assignments were for cyber security and network fundamentals. For cyber security, I am required to perform a comprehensive cybersecurity risk assessment for the same organisation that was in the fictional scenario in assignment one. I am also required to perform a demonstration of three mitigation tools, showing how each work to address specific risks. For the networking assignment, I am required to create a network plan for a different fictional scenario, including an analysis of the network requirements, a logical plan of the network, and diagrams showing the physical and logical topology of the network. I also have to perform a demonstration of a server-client network that I built myself, run on the virtual machines in the network lab. I am confident in my ability to do the written part of these assignments, however the practical element will be a challenge, since I have never done any networking practical before.



Entry 9

Another set of assignments were released, this time for programming fundamentals, and web application development. The programming assignment requires me to produce a single, large, object orientated programming solution for a test drive booking system. I had to design, test, and deploy a software artefact which would allow the hypothetical staff members to manage and oversee the car test drives the customers have booked. The web application development assignment requires me to design and develop a front-end prototype solution for the same existing website as the first assignment. I am confident in my ability to do these assignments, since the web application assignment is similar to what I had to do in my final exam in the T-Level course, and for the both assignments, they are the areas I have the most knowledge and skills in.



Entry 10

Another assignment was released, this one is for data science. The assignment requires me to select a domain, find/generate data based on that domain, design database schema, then use SQL to create and populate a database, then show the data using SQL queries. Next, I had to read the SQL database into a Pandas Data Frame, perform statistical analysis on the data frame, clean the data frame, produce visualisations from the data within the data frame, then use a machine learning model to make predictions based on the data. I am mostly confident in my ability to do this assignment, since I have done data visualisation and SQL before, however the aspect of machine learning is new to me, therefore there will be some challenges. Despite this, I will try hard to complete this assignment to the best of my ability.



Conclusion

Throughout my first year of study, I have developed a broad range of academic, technical, and professional skills across all modules. At the beginning of the year, I felt most confident in programming and web development due to my previous experience during my T-Level studies, while cyber security, networking, and some aspects of data science were areas where I lacked confidence. However, over the course of the year, I have significantly improved my understanding in these weaker areas through assignments, practical tasks, and independent research.

Although there are still areas I would like to improve further, particularly networking as well as cyber security, I am much more confident in my abilities than I was at the start of the year. Overall, this year has helped me become a more independent learner and has provided a strong foundation for the remainder of my degree and future career within the technology industry.
